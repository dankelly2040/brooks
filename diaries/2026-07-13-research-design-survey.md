# Initial mobile shoe-commerce design survey

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Agent:** Codex
**System:** Research
**Scope:** Select native-commerce reference apps and define the initial design evaluation rubric.

## Outcome

Created LLP 0001 with Brooks as the canonical brand and scope reference, plus
Nike, Zappos, adidas, and GOAT as complementary native-app benchmarks. Defined
a weighted rubric centered on native polish, shoe decision confidence,
discovery, Brooks storytelling, product/cart flow, accessibility, and demo
impact.

## What worked well

Using first-party product pages and current App Store listings made each app's
intended strengths easy to distinguish. Pairing broad-catalog utility (Zappos)
with first-party sports brands (Nike and adidas) and premium sneaker presentation
(GOAT) produced a more balanced reference set than searching for one “best” app.

## Friction and blockers

Search-indexed Brooks pages exposed current content and navigation, but direct
page opening was intermittently unavailable to the research tool. Store
descriptions also communicate feature sets better than interaction quality.

## What was hard

Public descriptions cannot establish the quality of gestures, motion, loading,
error handling, or cross-screen continuity. Those need a hands-on walkthrough
of current installed builds before detailed visual implementation.

## Expo and Exact comparison

Not observed. This task established product references before either app was
implemented.

## Improvement ideas

Create a repeatable capture template for hands-on app walkthroughs so agents can
compare the same screens and states without producing a large, unfocused mood
board.

## Follow-ups

Walk through home, category, search/filter, product detail, login, and cart in
each available reference app, then turn the strongest patterns into Brooks
wireframes or prototypes.
